I have written a book: what now?

by Giovanni Casali, editorial coordinator

You have the manuscript: you are missing the rest. Publishing it with a professional package costs from 1,500 euros: cover, print and EPUB layout, upload to the stores, print on demand. No warehouse, no minimum print runs, and the rights remain yours.

First question, honestly: is the text really ready?

Almost no manuscript is, and that is not a flaw: it is the trade. Before the cover comes the editing: structure, rhythm, typos, the sentences that sound right to you because you wrote them. A professional eye tells you in one conversation what your text needs: sometimes a dusting, sometimes a rewrite. Better to know before printing.

The three roads to publication

The traditional publisher: you pay nothing, but it is selective, slow, and most of the rights go to them. Paid publishing: have them explain exactly what you are paying for, because you often pay a lot to get little. Professional self-publishing, our road: you pay for the work once, the book comes out dressed for the bookshop, and rights and revenues stay yours.

What the package includes

A professional cover, print layout, EPUB for ebooks, upload to the main stores, print on demand. From 1,500 euros. The book comes out wearing the same clothes as the books in the bookshop: the whole difference between a printed manuscript and a real book is here.

What you do NOT need

A garage print run: with print on demand, copies are printed when needed, five or five hundred. A warehouse. And above all: anyone promising you a bestseller. No one can promise that, and whoever does is selling the promise, not the book.

Quick questions

How long until the book is out?

If the text is ready, weeks, not months: the time for cover, layout and upload. If it needs editing, the calendar is built at the consultation.

Which stores does it go to?

The main online stores, in print and ebook. The book can be ordered from any bookshop and arrives home like any other.

How many copies do I have to buy?

None. Print on demand produces copies when someone orders them: no boxes in the garage, no investment in paper.
